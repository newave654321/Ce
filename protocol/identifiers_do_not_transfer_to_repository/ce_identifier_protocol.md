# .ce Identifier Protocol

**Protocol Name:** `.ce` Semantic Identifier Layer

**Purpose:**  
Provide a non-owned, equitable, and emergent identifier system for Ce-Block nodes and resources, enabling consistent expression, discovery, and fragment recall without reliance on identity, ownership, or monetary exchange.

## Definition
- `.ce` identifiers are **semantic addresses** for resources, nodes, or expressions in the Ce ecosystem.  
- Identifiers **cannot be registered, bought, or sold**.  
- `.ce` serves as a **location-agnostic anchor** for fragments, nodes, and resource expressions.

## Resolution Mechanism
1. Nodes advertise `.ce` identifiers they can express.  
2. Requesting nodes resolve `.ce` identifiers using:
   - Node availability  
   - Constraint-driven discovery  
   - Optional participation signals (e.g., mobile devices, IoT nodes)  
3. Fragments of data or resources associated with a `.ce` identifier are retrieved from one or more available nodes.

## Equity Rules
- Access is **permissioned via availability and capability**, not ownership or account status.  
- Participation in `.ce` resolution is **voluntary and non-monetary**.  
- System discourages monopolization of identifiers or fragments.

## Integration with Ce-Block
- `.ce` identifiers can be referenced in Ce-Block transactions for:
  - Checkpointing expressed fragments  
  - Tracking resource interactions  
  - Linking ephemeral or persistent data across nodes  
- Ce-Block protocol ensures **non-coercive, high-safety handling** of `.ce` identifiers.

## Optional Extensions
- Node types (mobile, stationary, IoT) may advertise expression capacity.  
- Browser/OS-based resolvers may interpret `.ce` identifiers to retrieve resources from nearest or fastest nodes.
