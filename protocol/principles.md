# Principles

The Ce protocol is governed by the following principles.
All compliant implementations must preserve these principles.

---

## 1. Agreement Over Authority

No node possesses inherent authority.
Agreement arises from voluntary recognition, not enforcement.

---

## 2. Coherence Before Expansion

Systems must maintain coherence prior to scaling, optimization, or extension.
Growth that undermines coherence is non-compliant.

---

## 3. Identity Neutrality

Protocol behavior must not depend on identity.
Identifiers may be used, but identity must not confer privilege or control.

---

## 4. Recall Without Ownership

No node owns system memory.
Recall is achieved through recognized state, not possession.

---

## 5. Exit Without Penalty

Nodes must be able to disengage without coercion, loss of recognition, or punitive consequence.

---

These principles define the invariant constraints of the Ce protocol.
