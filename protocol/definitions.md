# Definitions

This document defines terms used throughout the Ce protocol.
All terms are normative unless explicitly stated otherwise.

---

**Agreement**
A voluntarily recognized alignment between participating nodes regarding a shared state.

**Coherence**
The condition in which system components remain structurally and temporally aligned such that recognized states persist without contradiction.

**Recall**
The ability of a system to reconstitute a previously recognized state without reliance on authority or ownership.

**Node**
Any participating entity capable of recognizing, recording, or responding to agreement.

**State**
A condition or configuration recognized by agreement among nodes.

**Identifier**
A symbolic reference used to distinguish nodes or states without asserting identity.

**Identity**
A contextual or external attribute of a node that is not required for protocol operation.

**Protocol**
A set of constraints and definitions that governs recognition, agreement, and recall.

---

These definitions are authoritative within the scope of the Ce protocol.
