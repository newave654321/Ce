# Coherence

Coherence is the structural condition that enables agreement and recall.

---

## Structural Coherence

Structural coherence exists when relationships between nodes and states are non-contradictory and mutually intelligible.

---

## Temporal Coherence

Temporal coherence exists when recognized states remain consistent across time without requiring centralized enforcement.

---

## Nested Coherence

Subsystems may maintain local coherence provided they do not violate higher-order coherence.

---

## Failure of Coherence

A system is incoherent if:

* States contradict prior recognized agreements
* Recognition depends on authority
* Recall requires identity or ownership

Loss of coherence invalidates agreement.
