# Logical Model (Reference Architecture)

This document describes a conceptual logical model for Ce-aligned systems.
It is illustrative and non-normative.

The logical model focuses on **relationships**, not control flow.

---

## Core Logical Elements

### Signals

Persistent indicators of system state.
Signals do not issue commands and do not require immediate response.

Examples:

* Carbon intensity
* Energy availability
* Participation density
* Agreement persistence

---

### Agreement States

Voluntary alignments formed in response to observed signals.
Agreement states may change, dissolve, or recur.

Agreement states are:

* Non-binding
* Time-aware
* Revisable

---

### Recall Context

A retained memory of prior agreement states.
Recall provides orientation rather than enforcement.

Recall never triggers automatic penalties or corrective actions.

---

## Logical Flow (Non-Sequential)

1. Signals persist over time
2. Participants observe signals
3. Agreements form or dissolve voluntarily
4. Agreement states are recalled
5. Recall informs future alignment

This flow is cyclical, not hierarchical.

---

## Constraints

* No central authority
* No mandatory identifiers
* No irreversible commitment

This model represents **one possible logical interpretation** of Ce.
